Cass is fine. The word comes later. What Cass gave up is harder to name:
the safety of not being involved. A certain smallness that felt like caution.

Alex recognizes it. Alex had that safety too, once. Eleven years of it.

---
**Craft note — Subplot Resolution:** The final subplot beat should echo the
protagonist's arc. Cass gave up the same thing Alex gave up — the protection
of not caring. Two characters who made the same choice for the same reason,
without having coordinated it. When a B-plot character mirrors the A-plot
character's transformation independently, it confirms that the theme is
real — it's not just one person's story, it's something true about people.
