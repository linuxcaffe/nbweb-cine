When VALE's people close in, Cass goes the wrong direction deliberately.
The direction that pulls attention away from Alex. No explanation. No time.

Alex doesn't know if Cass will be okay. Alex keeps moving, because that's
what Cass just paid for.

---
**Craft note — Subplot Climax / Ally Sacrifice:** Cass's choice answers the
trustworthiness question definitively — but too late for it to be comfortable.
The audience should feel the cost. Alex's choice to keep moving is not coldness.
It is the respect of accepting what was given. Note the structural position:
this happens just after the Second Plot Point, as Act 3 begins. The subplot
resolves its central question precisely when the main plot most needs Alex to
be free to act.
