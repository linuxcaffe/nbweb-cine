On the bridge, crossing. Cass tells Alex: VOSS is dead. Not missing — dead.
Three weeks ago. Which means the envelope was written before that. Which means
VOSS knew what was coming, and sent the message anyway, and chose Alex.

Alex had suspected. But suspecting and knowing are different things.

---
**Craft note — Midpoint Subplot Revelation:** The midpoint in the main plot
(false safety, bridge crossing) is mirrored in the subplot by this revelation.
It reframes the entire job. Alex isn't delivering for a living person — this is
a posthumous task. Cass's willingness to share this painful information is the
first real reason to trust them. Trust, in a subplot about trust, should be
earned through a cost. This is Cass's first cost.
