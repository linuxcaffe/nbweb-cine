The protagonist faces the antagonist with everything at stake.
The internal change must be expressed through external action.

VALE is at the destination. Cass is somewhere unknown. Alex arrives
and says the words out loud instead of handing anything over. There
was nothing to hand over. The confession was never on paper.

---
**Craft note — Climax (~90%):** The climax must resolve BOTH the external plot
(the delivery) and the internal arc (Alex's relationship to the past failure).
These should happen simultaneously through the same action — not in separate
scenes. If Alex wins the external battle but hasn't changed internally, it's
a hollow ending. If Alex changes internally but the external problem is ignored,
it's unsatisfying. The craft is making one action do both.
