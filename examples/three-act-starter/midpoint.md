Things seem to be going well. Or a revelation reframes everything seen so far.
The protagonist thinks they understand the situation. They don't.

Alex and Cass reach the river crossing. The far bank is clear. For a moment,
crossing the bridge feels like winning.

---
**Craft note — Midpoint (~50%):** Two types of midpoint: False Victory (protagonist
seems to win) and False Defeat (protagonist seems to lose). Both are false — that's
the point. The story turns here regardless. Act 2B will systematically undo whatever
2A built. If you wrote a False Victory, the fall lands harder because the audience
fell with the protagonist. If you wrote a False Defeat, the recovery in 2B feels
more earned.
