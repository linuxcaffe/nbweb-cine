The envelope is at Alex's usual drop. Unsigned — but VOSS's seal. Alex hasn't
seen that seal in eleven years. The payment attached is three times the rate.
The address is across the city, and the city shuts down in four hours.

Alex takes the job without reading the message. That's the first mistake.
It's also the first choice that feels like Alex.

(the recognition of VOSS's name, the impossibility of the situation) is one beat
later. This is the pull before the push. Alex chooses to pick up the envelope
before fully understanding what it means. That choice — made with incomplete
information, for obscure reasons — is the most revealing thing in Act 1.
