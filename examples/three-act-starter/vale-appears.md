First time, coincidence — same corner, same direction. Second time: not
coincidence. VALE doesn't approach, doesn't threaten, doesn't make eye
contact. Just mirrors Alex's route from half a block back.

Someone hired VALE for this. That means someone knows about the message
who wasn't supposed to.

---
**Craft note — Antagonist Introduction:** VALE's competence should be apparent
through precision, not menace. The protagonist recognizing a professional is
itself a character beat — it tells us what Alex has experience with. VALE's
goal (intercept the message) directly opposes Alex's goal (deliver it). A
good antagonist isn't evil — they're in the way, and they're good at it.
